Backup — In Case They Ask

Anticipated questions

01
What if the agent edits the gates so they pass?
Real gap in this minimal demo. The guardrail here is the prompt ("don't touch /gates") plus a human reviewing the diff. In a production version, the agent has no write access to /gates at all — that's a permissions problem, not a prompting one.
02
Why NetArchTest instead of just a linter?
A linter reads syntax and style. NetArchTest inspects the compiled assembly and verifies real dependency relationships between layers — whether the compiler actually generated a reference to another assembly, not just how the code looks.
03
Is this .NET-specific?
The pattern — specs plus gates plus a feedback loop — is stack-agnostic. Equivalents in other ecosystems: ArchUnit (Java), import-linter (Python), dependency-cruiser (JS/TS).
04
Why Greenfield, not Brownfield?
A deliberate scope choice for this week: validate the complete mechanism in the time available. Adapting a Brownfield project to SDD is a logical next step, not something skipped for being technically hard.
